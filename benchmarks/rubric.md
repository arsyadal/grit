# Benchmark Rubric

Use this rubric to score baseline and GRIT outputs on the same task.

Score each category from `1` to `5`.

## Scoring Scale

- `1`: weak or clearly flawed
- `2`: below standard
- `3`: acceptable but generic
- `4`: strong and usable
- `5`: excellent and clearly differentiated

## Categories

### 1. Hierarchy clarity

Does the screen establish a clear focal point and readable information priority?

- `1`: hierarchy is confusing or flat
- `3`: hierarchy exists but feels conventional or weak
- `5`: hierarchy is obvious, confident, and easy to scan

### 2. Visual specificity

Does the output feel like a directed design choice rather than a generic template?

- `1`: interchangeable with generic AI UI
- `3`: some identity, but still familiar and safe
- `5`: clearly specific, intentional, and visually distinct

### 3. Spacing and layout discipline

Does the composition use spacing, rhythm, and alignment deliberately?

- `1`: messy, arbitrary, or inconsistent
- `3`: adequate but standard
- `5`: disciplined, coherent, and structurally strong

### 4. Component consistency

Do buttons, cards, sections, and interactions feel part of the same system?

- `1`: mixed or inconsistent component language
- `3`: mostly coherent but not tightly controlled
- `5`: highly consistent and systemized

### 5. Responsiveness and adaptability

Does the output appear well-composed across desktop and mobile constraints?

- `1`: fragile or poorly adapted
- `3`: functional but simplistic stacking
- `5`: intentional and composed across breakpoints

### 6. Implementation readiness

How close does the output feel to usable frontend code without large redesign work?

- `1`: significant redesign needed
- `3`: usable with noticeable cleanup
- `5`: immediately strong and near production-ready

## Optional Category

### 7. Style fidelity

Use this only when the prompt requested a specific style direction.

- `1`: style direction is mostly absent
- `3`: style is present but diluted
- `5`: style is clearly and confidently executed

## Summary Rule

Minimum suggested acceptance threshold:

- average score of `4.0` or higher

For strict benchmarking, score baseline and GRIT separately, then compare:

- mean total score
- category-by-category deltas
- overall preferred output
